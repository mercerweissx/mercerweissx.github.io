## The Author 
I'm a hobbyist computer programmer based in Saint Louis, Missouri. I deeply enjoy the process of studying a specific subject or problem and synthesizing my findings into a written work. This site hosts these writings and allows me to share them more easily.

Beyond learning, I love writing Python and tinkering with operating systems. I'm on the autism spectrum and currently work in a non-technical field to support myself while keeping software as a personal passion. 

I hold a culturally-progressive and economically-conservative libertarian worldview, ensuring members of both major American political parties find me extremely frustrating to talk to. While I don't intend to write on explicitly political topics, my political views will nonetheless shape how I write about the decidedly non-political.

<br>

## The Content
This blog hosts my personal essays and research projects, largely focused on either facts I found interesting enough to write about or my own musings on various technical subjects. I also intend to use this blog to showcase programming projects which I consider personal achievements.

Despite my political leanings, I remain extremely skeptical toward the use of generative artificial intelligence for most purposes. I write neither prose nor programs with the help of AI tooling, and have no intention of doing so in the future. Bite me, futurists.
