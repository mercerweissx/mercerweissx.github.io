---
title: College Was Always Vocational
description: Whether we like it or not.
date: 2026-07-20
layout: post
---
Within the United States today, there exists one universally accepted reason for students to pursue higher education: for better jobs. College has become nearly synonymous with economic mobility. High schoolers are shown graphs of average lifetime income by level of education, incoming freshman are advised to pick a major based on their anticipated career, and, when asked why they chose to attend college, most students respond with primarily economic reasons. It seems a given that, in addition to whatever personal motivations you have for going to college, your _main_ reason is expected to be career-related. 

Some people take an opposing view: college isn't about getting a job, it's about getting an _education_. Your major, they say, isn't nearly as important as your having a degree to begin with, because a degree's value lies in the time management and critical thinking skills it develops independent of field-specific knowledge.
